The tension

Healthcare wants GenAI.
Compliance won't let it in.

Clinicians have a use case waiting at every desk. The compliance officer has a stack of reasons to say no — and most of them are valid. The result is a quiet stalemate, and a quieter problem underneath it.

The demand
Clinicians want AI now
  • Summarize discharge notes in seconds, not minutes
  • Draft referral letters from rough notes
  • Spot abnormal trends in lab panels at a glance
  • Translate patient instructions into 5+ languages
The wall
Compliance says "not yet"
  • PHI exposure: every prompt is a potential leak
  • HIPAA penalties: up to $1.5M per violation per year
  • Vendor BAAs: slow, expensive, often blocking
  • No audit trail = no defense in a breach
Result: shadow AI. Clinicians paste PHI into public chatbots — and no one is watching.
The insight

Don't trust the model with PHI.
Never send it.

Almost every "responsible AI" approach assumes data has to reach the model first. We inverted the assumption.

Three Human Gates

PHI findings, sanitized text, and AI output each require explicit human approval before the next step. Nothing gets exported without all three.

Browser-Side Scrub

Detection and redaction run client-side. PHI literally cannot reach the cloud — by architecture, not by promise.

Audit by Default

Every action timestamped. Exportable as a compliance log. Defensible in any HIPAA review, with no extra steps for the clinician.
